1. Desirability

Critical Assumption

Customer-facing teams will trust AI recommendations enough to change their daily workflow and make customer decisions based on them.

Why this is critical

The briefing emphasizes that Pulse AI goes beyond a simple red/yellow/green health score by explaining what happened, why it happened, and what to do next. However, Customer Success Managers and Sales Managers remain accountable for renewals and revenue, so they may hesitate to rely on AI unless its reasoning is transparent.

Validation Metric

  • AI recommendation acceptance rate
  • Weekly active users
  • Percentage of AI-generated actions executed
  • User trust/NPS for AI insights

2. Viability

Critical Assumption

Detecting customer health risks earlier will create measurable business value by reducing churn and increasing expansion revenue.

Why this is critical

Pulse AI's value proposition is built on helping organizations reduce churn, improve retention, and accelerate revenue growth. If earlier insights don't translate into measurable business outcomes, customers may struggle to justify the investment.

Validation Metric

  • Churn reduction (%)
  • Renewal rate improvement
  • Expansion revenue generated
  • ARR protected
  • Time to intervention

3. Feasibility

Critical Assumption

Enterprise customer data from CRM, billing, support, product analytics, and monitoring tools can be integrated into a single, reliable customer health model.

Why this is critical

The product's core promise is to unify fragmented customer signals into one explained health view. That promise depends on reliable integrations, consistent data quality, and accurate identity resolution across systems.

Validation Metric

  • Integration success rate
  • Percentage of customer records successfully unified
  • AI insight accuracy
  • Data freshness
  • Source coverage across connected systems
